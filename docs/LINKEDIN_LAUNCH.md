Five agents, one ranch, $4.17 a week.

I just submitted **SkyHerd** to Anthropic's Built with Opus 4.7 hackathon — a
nervous system for working land.

A cow can be sick for 72 hours before anyone notices. A coyote can take three
calves in a night. Ranchers on 50,000 acres lose two hours every day just
checking water tanks from a truck. So I built the ranch that checks itself.

**Five Claude Managed Agents:**
- **FenceLineDispatcher** — predator shows up, drone launches, deterrent plays, rancher gets a call.
- **HerdHealthWatcher** — flags pinkeye, mastitis, and early illness from trough-camera frames.
- **PredatorPatternLearner** — maps crossings across nights, sessions persist.
- **GrazingOptimizer** — moves cattle before a hailstorm hits.
- **CalvingWatch** — pages when a cow is about to labor. Priority urgency.

Each agent pauses its own billing between events. The cost ticker literally
freezes. Idle-pause is a feature, not a side-effect.

Every tool call signs into an Ed25519 Merkle attestation chain. Year two of the
business model is an LRP insurance rider underwritten on water-reliability data
from that chain.

Sim-first. Reproducible in a fresh clone in under three minutes. MIT.

Built with Opus 4.7, Python, FastAPI, React, MAVSDK, MegaDetector V6, and a
cowboy-persona voice called Wes that I cloned in ElevenLabs.
