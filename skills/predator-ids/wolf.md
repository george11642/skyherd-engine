# Mexican Gray Wolf (Canis lupus baileyi)

## When to load

- Drone footage shows a large canid (>60 lb) in the Gila/Blue Range recovery area of western NM.
- A livestock kill shows evidence consistent with wolf predation (multiple bites, entire animal consumed over multiple days).
- Ranch is located in Catron, Grant, Hidalgo, or Socorro county NM (primary recovery zone).

## Summary

The Mexican gray wolf is the most legally protected predator in the SkyHerd operational area. Reintroduced by USFWS starting 1998 under the Mexican Wolf Recovery Program, the wild population in the US was 241 individuals as of January 2024. Any confirmed livestock kill by a Mexican wolf triggers a USFWS investigation and potential depredation compensation through the USFWS program or the Defenders of Wildlife compensation fund. Ranchers may NOT take lethal action against a wolf without a specific USFWS permit even in the act of attacking livestock. This makes rapid, correct ID and reporting critical.

## Key facts

- **Weight**: 60–80 lb (27–36 kg); slightly smaller than Great Plains gray wolf. Larger than coyote (20–50 lb) and LGD breeds may overlap.
- **Size**: shoulder height 26–32 inches; body length (without tail) 4–5 ft.
- **Coloration**: brownish-gray with tan and black markings; rusty tones on flanks and legs. Distinguished from coyote by larger size, blockier head, and broader chest.
- **Thermal signature**: larger than coyote; more massive torso; broader head visible on thermal. Pack movement is most reliable identifier — 3–11 wolves moving together in linear or spread formation.
- **Gait**: loping trot covers ground quickly; wolves travel 15–30 miles per night. Straight-line travel at 5 mph trot.
- **Vocalizations**: prolonged sustained howl (2–5 seconds per note) vs. coyote's shorter yip-howl. Group howl is unmistakable. Audio sensor + location metadata is important evidence for USFWS.
- **USFWS recovery area**: Mexican Wolf Experimental Population Area (MWEPA) covers Apache, Gila, and Cibola National Forests in AZ and NM; roughly south of I-40, west of the Rio Grande. Catron and Grant counties NM are core range.
- **Pack structure**: 3–11 individuals; territorial; packs maintain 50–100 sq mile home ranges.
- **Kill sign**: multiple bites to hindquarters and flanks (pack hunting); large portions of carcass consumed; adult cattle are attacked (unlike coyotes). Bite force higher than coyote; bones sometimes consumed.
- **Livestock compensation**: USFWS pays market value for confirmed wolf kills. Defenders of Wildlife supplementary fund available for "probable" kills. Documentation required: photos, veterinary necropsy, GPS coordinates.
- **Legal**: Endangered Species Act, Section 7 and 9. Lethal take without permit = federal felony. Livestock producers CAN use nonlethal deterrents freely.

## Decision rules

```
IF large canid (>60 lb apparent size on thermal) in Catron/Grant county:
  → Presumptive wolf; Tier 3 call rancher immediately
  → Activate deterrents; drone to 90m AGL
  → Record GPS, timestamp, thermal footage; this is USFWS evidence

IF pack movement detected (3+ canids traveling together):
  → Tier 3 regardless of county; pack = wolf or very large feral dog pack
  → Do NOT activate lethal deterrents (none exist on SkyHerd); acoustic only

IF wolf confirmed within 200m of livestock:
  → Tier 4 immediate; rancher on-property; call USFWS NM wolf hotline
  → Continue drone monitoring; document everything

IF livestock kill with characteristics of wolf attack (multiple bites, flanks):
  → Call rancher Tier 3; do NOT disturb carcass
  → USFWS Mexican Wolf reporting hotline: 1-888-459-9653
  → Document: GPS, photos, all animal bite locations, time discovered

IF howling detected at night in western NM counties:
  → Log audio; location metadata; add to PredatorPatternLearner dossier
  → No immediate escalation unless visual confirm follows

NEVER confuse wolf with LGD:
  → Cross-reference livestock-guardian-dogs.md; LGDs are territorial and stay near herd; wolves travel and approach
```

## Escalation / handoff

- **Tier 3**: confirmed wolf on property.
- **Tier 4**: kill confirmed or wolf inside herd.
- USFWS Mexican Wolf Reporting: 1-888-459-9653.
- NM Livestock Board (for kill compensation documentation): 505-841-6161.
