# New World Screwworm (Cochliomyia hominivorax)

## When to load

- Vision agent or rancher inspection identifies larvae inside a wound on any livestock.
- HerdHealthWatcher flags an animal repeatedly rubbing or returning to a wound site.
- Any open wound check in southern NM cattle within 50 miles of the US–Mexico border.

## Summary

New World Screwworm (*Cochliomyia hominivorax*) was eradicated from the US mainland in 1966 via sterile insect technique (SIT). USDA-APHIS re-declared the pest endemic in parts of Mexico in June 2025 after cases of US livestock infestation along the Texas and New Mexico border were confirmed. Feeder cattle import restrictions from Mexico were imposed November 2025. This is a **reportable, federally regulated pest**. Any confirmed or suspected case must be reported to APHIS within 24 hours. NM ranches within 100 miles of the Mexican border are in the highest-risk zone.

## Key facts

- **Fly**: metallic blue-green blow fly, 8–10mm. Lays 200–400 eggs in batches at edges of open wounds, natural body orifices, or even intact mucous membranes.
- **Larvae**: creamy white maggots, 1–15mm; bore into live tissue (obligate parasite on warm-blooded animals — unlike other blow flies that use dead tissue). Head-end visible under wound surface.
- **Wound types at risk**: fresh cuts, navel of newborn calves, dehorning wounds, castration sites, tick-bite lesions, wire scratches.
- **Clinical signs**:
  - Larvae visible in wound — cream-white, head-down in tissue.
  - Foul odor from wound.
  - Animal rubbing wound repeatedly; excessive tail switching.
  - Secondary bacterial infection common within 48 hrs.
  - Untreated: larvae can consume 10–12g of tissue per day; death in 7–14 days.
- **Season in NM**: April–October peak (warm months); theoretically year-round in Mexico borderland.
- **Regulatory status (2026)**: APHIS emergency order in effect; all suspect cases must be reported to NM Livestock Board within 24 hrs and APHIS within 24 hrs.
- **Import restrictions (Nov 2025)**: feeder cattle from Mexico require screwworm treatment certificate and APHIS inspection at border crossing.
- **Treatment**: manual removal of larvae; saturate wound with coumaphos or permethrin-based wound spray; systemic doramectin injection. Vet confirmation recommended on first case.

## Decision rules

```
IF any larvae visible in open wound:
  → Tier 4 page rancher immediately
  → File APHIS report (APHIS Form VS 9-3) within 24 hrs
  → Isolate animal; contact NM Livestock Board (505-841-6161)
  → DO NOT wait for lab confirmation to report; presumptive report is required

IF fresh wound on animal within 50 miles of NM-Mexico border:
  → Increase wound inspection frequency; add to daily HerdHealthWatcher scan list

IF animal showing wound-rubbing + foul odor detected (sensor or rancher report):
  → Drone close-pass for visual; if larvae not visible, rancher manual inspection needed

IF newborn calf with wet navel OR recent castration/dehorning in summer:
  → Flag for daily navel/wound check; apply preventive wound spray
```

## Escalation / handoff

- **Mandatory APHIS report**: 24 hrs from first suspicion. Non-compliance is a federal violation.
- Contact NM State Veterinarian: 505-841-6161.
- Vet on-site: every confirmed case for treatment documentation and regulatory compliance.
