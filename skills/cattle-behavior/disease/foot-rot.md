# Foot Rot (Interdigital Necrobacillosis)

## When to load

- Lameness-indicators skill has flagged a gait score 3+ animal and foot inspection is warranted.
- Recent wet conditions or paddock rotation through muddy ground.
- Multiple animals in same paddock showing hind-limb lameness within a short window.

## Summary

Foot rot is a bacterial infection of the interdigital (between-the-toes) space caused by a synergistic combination of *Fusobacterium necrophorum* and *Bacteroides melaninogenicus*. Wet, muddy conditions create skin maceration that allows bacterial entry. In NM, cases cluster after the summer monsoon (July–September) and after spring snowmelt (March–April). The infection spreads quickly — an untreated cow can go from mild limp to non-weight-bearing in 3–5 days. Systemic antibiotics are effective if given early.

## Key facts

- **Causative agents**: *Fusobacterium necrophorum* (primary) + *Bacteroides melaninogenicus* (synergist). Both are anaerobes, normal rumen and soil flora.
- **Entry**: macerated interdigital skin from prolonged wetness; mechanical injury (rocks, wire, cactus spines) facilitates.
- **Incubation**: 3–5 days after exposure to wet/contaminated ground.
- **Clinical signs**:
  - Interdigital swelling (both claws equally swollen — distinguishes from simple claw trauma which affects one claw).
  - Symmetric swelling above coronary band.
  - Foul-smelling necrotic discharge from interdigital space.
  - Sudden onset severe lameness (gait score 3–5).
  - Fever 104–106°F common.
  - Animal typically holds leg slightly off ground or toe-touches only.
- **Differentiation from digital dermatitis (hairy heel warts)**: foot rot has interdigital swelling; DD has ulcerative lesion at heel without swelling.
- **Treatment**: systemic oxytetracycline (9 mg/kg, long-acting) OR trimethoprim-sulfadoxine. Topical antiseptic spray adjunct. Response within 24–48 hrs if caught early.
- **Herd incidence**: 5–10% of animals per year in wet years on NM range (higher in feedlot settings).
- **Prevention**: zinc footbath (zinc sulfate 10%), avoid prolonged wet-ground paddocks, rotate off saturated areas within 3 days.

## Decision rules

```
IF interdigital swelling bilateral + foul odor + gait score 3+:
  → Confirm foot rot; Tier 3 rancher call; antibiotic treatment today

IF gait score 4–5 with foot rot signs:
  → Tier 4; vet call if animal non-ambulatory or fever >105°F

IF >2 animals in same paddock within 5 days:
  → Tier 2 text rancher; paddock rotation off wet ground recommended
  → Zinc footbath at paddock exit

IF gait score 3 with single-claw swelling (no interdigital involvement):
  → More likely claw trauma; cross-reference lameness-indicators; rancher visual needed
```

## Escalation / handoff

- **Tier 2**: 2+ animals, paddock management advisory.
- **Tier 3**: any confirmed foot rot case; antibiotic treatment needed.
- **Tier 4**: non-ambulatory animal, high fever, no treatment access.
