# Calving Signs

## When to load

- CalvingWatch agent is active during the Mar–Apr calving season (NM spring calving).
- A cow in the third trimester is showing behavioral changes (restlessness, isolation, tail flagging).
- A calf has been partially delivered for >30 minutes with no progress.

## Summary

Calving is the single highest-mortality event in the beef production calendar. On NM spring-calving operations, 80–90% of calvings occur in March and April. CalvingWatch monitors behavioral precursors starting 2–3 weeks pre-partum. Normal unassisted delivery takes 30 minutes to 4 hours from active labor onset; anything beyond 4 hours in Stage 2 is dystocia until proven otherwise. A rancher or vet on scene within 30 minutes of a 4-hour Stage 2 window saves most difficult calves.

## Key facts

- **Stage 1 (early labor)**: uterine contractions begin; cow appears restless, paces, gets up and down frequently, isolates from herd. Duration 2–6 hrs. Not visible on external exam; behavioral cues only.
- **Stage 2 (active delivery)**: water sac visible at vulva → calf visible → calf delivered. Normal duration: 30–90 min in multiparous cows, up to 4 hrs in heifers (first calving).
- **Stage 3 (placenta)**: placenta passed within 8–12 hrs. Retained placenta (>12 hrs) requires vet intervention.
- Pre-labor signs visible on camera (onset 12–24 hrs before delivery):
  - Tail-head elevation ("tail flag") — ligaments soften; visible on drone dorsal view.
  - Udder filling/distension — wax dripping from teats in last 24 hrs.
  - Vulvar swelling and relaxation.
  - Restlessness: lying down and rising >4× per hour vs. normal 1–2×.
  - Isolation from herd: cow leaves grazing group and seeks cover or fence line.
- Normal calf presentation: front hooves and nose together (diving position). 95% of calves.
- Abnormal presentations requiring assistance: breach (rear first), head-back, one leg retained. Any presentation other than front-hooves-and-nose needs rancher within 30 min of Stage 2 onset.
- Heifer first-calving: expected 20–30% assisted delivery rate on NM beef operations (NM State Univ. Extension AG-413).
- Ambient temperature matters: calves born below 32°F need dry shelter and colostrum within 1 hr or hypothermia risk rises sharply.
- Colostrum window: calf must nurse within 4–6 hrs for full antibody transfer; immunoglobulin absorption drops to near zero by 24 hrs.
- Twins occur in ~2% of beef calvings; second calf often presents within 30–60 min of first. Twin delivery is a rancher-alert event.

## Decision rules

```
IF cow isolated from herd with tail-flag AND restlessness >4 rises/hr:
  → CalvingWatch enters active monitoring; page rancher Tier 2 (heads up, imminent)
  → drone position overhead; 5-min check interval

IF water sac visible:
  → Stage 2 confirmed; start timer
  → page rancher Tier 3 (be ready to assist)

IF Stage 2 timer > 90 min with no progress visible:
  → page rancher Tier 4 (dystocia possible — go now)

IF Stage 2 timer > 4 hrs:
  → page rancher Tier 4 + vet call; dystocia confirmed; calf at high risk without immediate assistance

IF presentation is rear hooves or single leg only:
  → page rancher Tier 4 immediately regardless of time elapsed

IF calf delivered, not standing within 30 min:
  → page rancher Tier 3; calf may need warming or colostrum assist

IF ambient temp <32°F at delivery:
  → append cold-weather flag to rancher page; "get calf to shelter fast"

IF Stage 3 placenta not passed >12 hrs post-delivery:
  → log for rancher; vet call may be needed for retained placenta

IF cow shows no pre-labor signs but is within 5 days of estimated due date:
  → increase check frequency to 30-min intervals; no page yet
```

## Escalation / handoff

- **Tier 2 (text)**: pre-labor signs confirmed; calving likely within 12–24 hrs.
- **Tier 3 (call)**: Stage 2 active; rancher should be on-property.
- **Tier 4 (immediate)**: Stage 2 >90 min no progress, abnormal presentation, calf down post-birth.
- Vet escalation: Stage 2 >4 hrs, retained placenta, twins with second not delivered in 1 hr.
